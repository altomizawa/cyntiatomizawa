#Collections

##FENCES
The Fences collection reflects my passion for bold, sculptural pieces - designed to appear substantial while using minimal gold. By integrating gemstones and intentional negative space, I created a visual rhythm that expands the silhouette without excess weight. This approach balances elegance and economy, allowing archiltectural forms to emerge from thoughtful material use.

##GEOMETRY
The core phylosophy behind this collection: sculptural metalwork takes center stage, while diamonds serve as subtle enhancers. By applying a matte finish, I introduced a refined contrast that allows the diamonds to quietly illuminate the geometry without overpowering it. 

##MAZE
The Maze collection was bonr from my fascination with the endless shapes and pathways found in mazes. Their parallel lines and geometric rhytim sparked the idea of transforming that visual language into jewelry. I began exploring how these lines could reconnect integrating gemstones as structural connectors, much like I had done in my earlier Fences Collection. This approach allowed me to create pieces that appear bold and expansive while using a reduced amount of metal, achieving visual impact through thoughtful design rather than weight.

##LUNAR
I inherited my love for pearls from my japanese grandmother. When I was ten, she gifted me a pair of Mikimoto earrings - my first introduction to the quiet power of this classic gemstone. Today, I bring that heritage into my work by reimagining pearls in contemporary silhouettes, creating pieces that feel both modern and timeless. 

##COLORS OF BRAZIL
The color palette for this collection was inspired by the Gothic churches I visited throughout Europe while living there. The luminous stained-glass windows - rich with deep hues and dramatic contrast - have always fascinated me, and their interplay of light and color became a guiding influence in these designs.
